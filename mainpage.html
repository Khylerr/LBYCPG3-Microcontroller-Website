What are Microcontrollers?

A microcontroller (MCU) is a compact, self-contained computer on a single microchip, designed to control specific tasks within an electronic system. Unlike a general-purpose computer, an MCU is dedicated to executing a single repetitive program efficiently. It integrates a CPU, Memory, and I/O Peripherals into one tiny package. They are the "brains" behind everyday devices, from microwave ovens to automotive braking systems.
