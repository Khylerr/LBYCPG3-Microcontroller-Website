Infineon Technologies

Infineon Technologies AG is a German semiconductor manufacturing and design company. It is the leading automotive semiconductor manufacturer globally. The company was spun-off from Siemens AG in 1999. Infineon employs roughly 57,000 employees, and in 2025, the company achieved sales of approximately €15 billion.

AURIX
  • AURIX is Infineon's high-performance automotive MCU family built around the proprietary TriCore architecture (32-bit CPU combining RISC, DSP, and microcontroller capabilities). Designed for safety-critical automotive applications, it supports ISO 26262 ASIL-D compliance with features like lockstep cores, ECC-protected memories, and hardware safety monitors.

XMC (Cross-Market Microcontrollers)
  • XMC is Infineon's scalable family of ARM Cortex-M based MCUs (M0, M4, and M7 cores) optimized for industrial applications requiring precise real-time control. Key strengths include specialized peripherals for motor control (POSIF, CCU8), power conversion (high-resolution PWMs), and industrial connectivity (CAN, EtherCAT).
 Download
PSoC (Programmable System-on-Chip)
  • Originally developed by Cypress Semiconductor (acquired by Infineon in 2020), PSoC integrates an ARM Cortex-M core with programmable analog and digital fabric on a single chip. This unique architecture allows designers to customize peripheral functions, such as op-amps, ADCs, timers, or communication interfaces, via reconfigurable hardware blocks rather than fixed silicon.
