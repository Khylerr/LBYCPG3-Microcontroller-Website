ESP32 Microcontroller
  • ESP32 is a family of low-cost, energy-efficient microcontrollers that integrate both Wi-Fi and Bluetooth capabilities. These chips feature a variety of processing options, including the Tensilica Xtensa LX6 microprocessor available in both dual-core and single-core variants, the Xtensa LX7 dual-core processor, or a single-core RISC-V microprocessor.
  • With a rich set of peripherals including capacitive touch sensors, Hall effect sensors, SD card interfaces, Ethernet, high-speed SPI, UART, I2S, and I2C, the ESP32 is designed to meet the needs of a wide range of applications. It is widely used in IoT projects, wearable electronics, and smart home devices.

ESP8266 Microcontroller
  • The ESP8266 is a low-cost Wi-Fi microchip, with built-in TCP/IP networking software, and microcontroller capability. It’s a 32-bit System-on-a-Chip (SoC) with built-in Wi-Fi and TCP/IP networking. Widely used for IoT, it features an 80- 160MHz CPU, 16 GPIOs, and 2.4 GHz Wi-Fi (802.11 b/g/n), often programmed via Arduino IDE or MicroPython.
  • The ESP8266 is popular for its affordability and ease of use, making it a favorite among hobbyists and developers for creating connected devices, home automation systems, and various IoT applications.

ESP32-P4 Microcontroller
  • The ESP32-P4 is a high-performance microcontroller that features a dual-core RISC-V processor running at up to 400 MHz, integrated memory controllers, multiple communication interfaces (Ethernet, USB, CAN, SPI, I2C), and support for various operating systems including Linux.
  • It is designed for applications requiring high performance and low power consumption in industrial and automotive environments.
