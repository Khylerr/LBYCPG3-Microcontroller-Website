Texas Instruments

Texas Instruments (TI) is an American multinational semiconductor company headquartered in Dallas, Texas. Founded in 1930, it specializes in designing, manufacturing, and selling analog and embedded processing chips. TI is one of the top 10 semiconductor companies worldwide, focusing on developing analog chips and embedded processors, which account for over 80% of its revenue.

MSP430
  • The MSP430 16-bit ultra-low-power MCU family designed for battery-operated embedded applications where energy efficiency is critical. Typical uses include portable medical devices, smart sensors, utility meters, and industrial monitoring systems. Its standout feature is sub-microamp sleep currents with fast wake-up times.

C2000 Real-Time MCUs
  • The C2000 is a 32-bit MCUs optimized for precision real-time control applications requiring deterministic, low-latency response. They excel in motor control, digital power supplies, solar inverters, and industrial automation due to integrated hardware accelerators for PWM generation, high-resolution ADCs, and control law computation.

Tiva C Series (ARM Cortex-M)
  • The Tiva ARM MCU’s are 32-bit MCUs built on the ARM Cortex-M4F core (with floating-point unit), including the TM4C123x and TM4C129x series. These general-purpose MCUs target embedded applications requiring connectivity, featuring integrated USB, Ethernet, CAN, and multiple serial interfaces.

Hercules Safety ARM MCU
  • Hercules Safety Microcontrollers are ARM-based MCUs (Cortex-R4/R5 and Cortex-M3/M4 cores) engineered specifically for functional safety-critical systems. They include hardware safety features like dual-core lockstep operation, ECC-protected memories, built-in self-test (BIST), and error signaling to meet certifications such as ISO 26262.
